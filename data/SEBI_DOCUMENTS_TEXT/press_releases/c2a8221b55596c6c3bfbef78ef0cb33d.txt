Shri Anantharaman takes charge as Whole Time Member, SEBI
Shri G.Anantharaman has taken charge as Whole Time Member of Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) on December 15, 2004. He shall be in charge of Investigations, Surveillance, Market Regulations and Derivatives functions at SEBI. Prior to this assignment, Shri Anantharaman retired as Chief Commissioner of Income Tax, Mumbai on June 30, 2004.
An officer of the Indian Revenue Service (IRS) 1968, Shri Anantharaman has held various senior positions in the Government of India. He served FERA and held several positions as in charge of investigation of tax cases, which includes the tenure of Director General (Investigation), Mumbai from 2002 to 2004. Shri Anantharaman was a Member of the Justice Kania Committee appointed to advise SEBI on corporatisation and demutualisation of Stock Exchanges in March 2002. He was also a Member of the Rosha Committee appointed by Delhi High Court which was constituted to enquire into the hospital equipment import scam across the country in 1996. Author of several articles on vigilance related matters, Shri Anantharaman, a honorary member of the Central Advisory Council of the Central Vigilance Commission, was also Director (Vigilance) in Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Limited (HPCL).
Widely traveled, Shri Anantharaman has attended various seminars on taxation, money laundering etc. in various countries.
Mumbai
December 17, 2004
