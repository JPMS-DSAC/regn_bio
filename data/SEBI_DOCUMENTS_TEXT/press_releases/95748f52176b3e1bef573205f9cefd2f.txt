January 22, 2004 PR No.12/2004
 Sub.: Amendment of SEBI (Informal Guidance) Scheme, 2003
  
The SEBI (Informal Guidance) Scheme, 2003 was issued on 24.6.2003, providing for the listed companies and registered capital market intermediaries to request for informal guidance regarding applicability of laws and regulations administered by SEBI in the form of no action letters or interpretive letters from SEBI.
Pursuant to suggestions received from various quarters and SEBI’s experience in the working of the Informal Guidance Scheme, it has been decided to expand the scope of the Scheme to enable the following persons (in addition to listed companies and SEBI-registered intermediaries) to seek informal guidance under the Scheme:
 

Any company which intends to get any of its securities listed and which has filed either a listing application with any stock exchange or a draft offer document with the Board or the Central Listing Authority;
Any mutual fund trustee company or asset management company;
Any acquirer or prospective acquirer under the Securities and Exchange Board of India (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 1997.

Accordingly, the SEBI (Informal Guidance) Scheme, 2003 has been amended on 21st January, 2004 to give effect to the above. The text of the Amendments are available on the SEBI website www.sebi.gov.in.
