PRESS RELEASE
December 03, 2003 PR No.290/2003

 
DIVISION OF CORPORATE RESTRUCTURING 
In the matter of proposed acquisition of shares of Comp-U-Learn Tech India Limited
 
Shri Mukesh Kumar Aggarwal [hereinafter referred to as ‘Acquirer’], together with associates hold 5,04,000 equity shares of Rs.10/- each representing 5.04% of the share capital of Comp–U–Learn Tech India Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as the “Target company”). The acquirer proposed to acquire 18,63,827 equity shares of the Target Company representing 18.63% the total paid up capital of the Target Company from Shri KRC Shekhar at a price of Rs.3.50/- per share.
 
The Acquirer made an application to SEBI under Regulation 4(2) of SEBI (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as “Regulations”) seeking exemption in terms of Regulation 3(1)(l) from the applicability of Regulation 10 and 12 of the Regulations with regard to aforesaid proposed acquisition. 
 
This application was referred to the Takeover Panel. The Takeover Panel did not recommend grant of exemption sought. 
 
The acquirer failed to prove that he is indeed a promoter of the company and that the transfer is inter se between the promoters. 
 
SEBI passed an order dated 29.11.2003 rejecting the application for exemption. The full text of the Order is available on the SEBI website at www.sebi.gov.in.
